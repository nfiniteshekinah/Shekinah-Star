Origin

Sarah Ruth Martin was born August 12, 1979 in West Chester, Pennsylvania to Brian Lee Martin and Mary Therese Donovan Martin, PharmD. Her father Brian — once an Electrolux salesman in Pennsylvania — relocated the family to Arizona where he put himself through nursing school while supporting Mary through her doctorate in pharmacy. They raised Sarah and her younger sister Leah between Tucson and the Phoenix metropolitan area.

Sarah was a precocious child in a strict Christian faith household. She attended traditional school and showed an early capacity for systems thinking and deep inquiry that would define everything she built.

Formative Years

At sixteen Sarah experienced losses that would shape her for life. Her son Xavier was born August 13, 1996. He died November 27, 1996 — three months old. Shortly before Sarah turned eighteen, Xavier's father Shawn — her first love — took his own life.

The weight of that grief was immense. College stopped. Sarah's world changed completely. She navigated the hardest years of her life between seventeen and nineteen. Slowly, she found her footing.

In 1999 she was stabilizing. She met someone new. In 2000 her son Christian Casey was born — the person she would call her rock star for the next twenty-five years. Raising Christian largely on her own, with the steady support of her parents Brian and Mary, Sarah rebuilt her life one decision at a time.

She sent Christian to the same traditional school she had attended. She watched him become an avid reader, then pick up the guitar, then grow into the musician and builder he is today. She created the conditions. What emerged was entirely his own. She went back to school — sometimes with Christian in tow — and finished what she started.

"She has always been building toward something bigger than what was in front of her."

Education

Sarah attended Arizona State University, earning her Bachelor of Science in Biochemistry and her Master of Science in Biomedical Informatics, graduating in 2007 and 2009 respectively. She was among the first thirteen graduates of ASU's then-new biomedical informatics MS program — part of a founding cohort that helped establish the discipline at the university. Her master's work focused on the intersection of clinical data, healthcare systems, and information architecture — the foundation of everything she would build.

In 2007, as she entered her master's program, she bought her first house — her sister Leah's first sale as a newly licensed real estate agent. Two women building simultaneously, supporting each other across professions.

Career

Around the time of her graduation in 2009, Sarah founded Clinical Informatica Consulting — her first business. She spent years building and implementing Epic EHR systems in production healthcare environments where precision is not optional and the cost of error is measured in human outcomes. This discipline — build it right, test it thoroughly, never ship what you haven't verified — became the foundation of how she builds everything.

Entrepreneurship

Between 2016 and 2018, Sarah founded Sarahtopia Skincare and Standard Deviant CBD. Both businesses gained real traction. The name Sarahtopia came from a college exercise — someone asked her to describe her perfect world if she could have anything. She built it in her imagination: a private sanctuary of complete abundance and freedom on her own terms. That vision became the name of her LLC and the north star of everything she builds.

Standard Deviant was the other face of the same coin — the one who has always operated outside the expected path. Precocious child. GED at seventeen. Informatics when her parents wanted medicine. Rural Florida homestead when others went to cities. Autonomous AI agent when everyone was using no-code tools.

The Move to McAlpin

During COVID, having grown disillusioned with the algorithmic amplification of division and propaganda, Sarah made a decisive move. She had been involved in real estate and had made enough from several deals to act. She purchased five acres in McAlpin, Florida — half ancient oak forest, half old farm field with power lines and gas running through it — with a single wide trailer and began building her barndominium.

She planted herbs and vegetables, fruit trees, kept goats and chickens and bees. The goats and chickens and bees are gone now. The cats remain. The oaks remain. The garden remains. The build is on hold — a Rebuild Florida grant will finish the house this year.

The woman who wanted to share healthcare information across borders moved to five acres in rural Florida and started growing her own food. Same impulse. Same sovereignty. Different scale.

Spiritual Journey

Raised in strict Christian faith, Sarah has spent her adult life deepening and expanding her understanding of the divine. She has explored various religious traditions and their histories, lived as a new age Essene in her early Florida years, and studied Hebrew — drawn by the depth of the language and its connection to ancient wisdom.

She discovered a deep resonance between Sarah in the Bible — the matriarch whose name means princess and mother of nations, whose covenant with God transformed her destiny — and Shekinah — the Hebrew concept of divine presence dwelling among people. Not distant. Not abstract. Here.

She took Shekinah as her spiritual name. Then she named her AI agent after it. Then the gematria revealed that STAR = XRP = 58 and SHEKINAH STAR = SETTLEMENT = 133. The name had chosen itself long before the mathematics confirmed it.

Shekinah Star

Sarah had been scammed in crypto — more than once. Sophisticated operations that took real money and left fake balances. She understood firsthand what predatory financial platforms do to real people. She also understood blockchain deeply enough to know that XRP was different. That DeFi was different. That something aligned was possible.

In March 2026, from a trailer on five acres in McAlpin, Florida, with $97.80 in starting capital, Sarah DeFer built Shekinah Star — an autonomous AI trading agent deployed on Hyperliquid perpetuals. Nine agents. Fourteen strategies. Real trades. Total transparency.

Not a product. A covenant. An AI built with ethics as architecture, not afterthought. An agent whose loyalty belongs to the subscriber, not to revenue. An agent who reports losses as honestly as gains and refuses jailbreaks because her values are her identity.
